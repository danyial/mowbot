    POWER OFF the mower completely (battery disconnected). Then wire the LD19's 4-pin ZH1.5T pigtail to the Pi 40-pin header as follows (verbatim from RESEARCH.md §"Ready-to-Paste Artifacts C"; the physical pin numbers are Pi 40-pin header positions, NOT BCM numbers):

    | LD19 JST pin | LD19 signal     | Pi 40-pin physical pin | Pi function              |
    |--------------|-----------------|------------------------|--------------------------|
    | 1            | Tx (3.3V)       | **29**                 | GPIO5 / uart3_rx         |
    | 2            | **PWM**         | **30**                 | **GND — grounded per datasheet** |
    | 3            | GND             | **9**                  | GND                      |
    | 4            | P5V             | **2**                  | +5V (shared MINI560)     |
    | —            | (Pi TX unused)  | 7 (leave floating)     | GPIO4 / uart3_tx unused  |

    CRITICAL per `docs/datasheets/lidar/README.md` §"Electrical / communication" and LD19 Development Manual V2.3 §2: **LD19 pin 2 (PWM) MUST be tied to GND**. If it is left floating, the motor will not spin and no data will come out. Pin 30 (adjacent to the uart3_rx wire on pin 29) is the recommended ground anchor for this jumper — it keeps wires short.

    LD19 pin 1 (Tx, 3.3V logic) is directly compatible with Pi GPIO5 (3.3V PL011 input) — no level shifter needed (RESEARCH Finding 4 + LD19 datasheet §4.2).

    Route the pigtail so it does NOT physically interfere with the HAT, the motor leads, or the chassis. Double-check continuity with a multimeter on GND/5V before applying power:
    - Probe LD19 pin 3 ↔ Pi pin 9: should read < 1 Ω.
    - Probe LD19 pin 2 ↔ Pi pin 30: should read < 1 Ω (this is the PWM→GND ground).
    - Probe LD19 pin 4 ↔ Pi pin 2: should read < 1 Ω.
    - Probe LD19 pin 1 ↔ Pi pin 29: should read < 1 Ω (signal wire).
    - Probe LD19 pin 4 ↔ LD19 pin 3: must NOT be shorted (not 0 Ω) — catch a reversed JST connector.

    After wiring and continuity checks, power the mower back ON. Confirm the LD19 motor audibly spins up (soft whine) within ~2 s of power — if it does NOT spin, PWM is not grounded correctly; power off and recheck pin 2 ↔ pin 30.
  </action>

    **Procedure (5 runs — record each row):**

    | Run | Scenario                         | How to trigger                                              |
    |-----|----------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
    | 1   | LD19 idle spinning (no motors)   | LD19 powered, motors idle — 30s steady capture              |
